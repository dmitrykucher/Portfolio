Product Designer · Spatial Computing & AI

For nine years I designed the interaction layer of Spectacles — the spatial UI primitives, input models, and platform frameworks that shipped across multiple generations of AR hardware. Specs
Navigation

Walking navigation for Specs, with the map rendered in world space. I settled on a ~45° tilt after testing the range — laid flat it fights Specs' ray-cast targeting, stood upright it tires the hand. Search is multimodal — hand input or voice; speaking shows a live waveform, then an AI returns a summary with the results. The map stays world-locked while you browse, then becomes a head-locked minimap the moment you start walking, so guidance follows you without holding your attention. I validated these decisions in-headset by generating interactive prototypes with Cursor wired into Lens Studio over MCP. Owned end to end; early preview at AWE 2026.

Video: Snap Newsroom · AWE 2026 keynote · © Snap Inc.

Measure

Spatial measurement for distances and volumes, designed around furniture fitting. I committed to manual marker placement after auto-detection proved not precise enough to trust for real fitting decisions — reliability over automation. Results read as numbers anchored in world space above each line. The same Cursor-into-Lens-Studio MCP loop let me test how placing markers by hand actually felt and cut what didn't work. Owned end to end; early preview at AWE 2026.

Spectacles 2024
Container

Spectacles had no consistent system-level component for moveable UI. The Container came out of that gap: a frame that wraps UI elements and makes them moveable in AR space. It became the foundation for all system UI on Spectacles.

Spatial Targeting

The original cursor rendered at a fixed depth and jumped abruptly when hitting UI — users couldn't build a mental model for how targeting worked. I introduced a proximity-aware cursor that fades in when targeting an interactable object and disappears otherwise, added a visible ray, and defined a depth constraint system. Validated with a controlled prototype study: the new design produced significantly more successful hits.

Multiplayer system flow

Redesigned the system-level multiplayer UX on Spectacles — moving colocated session discovery from a per-experience feature to a platform capability. Users can now find and join active nearby sessions across all experiences from a dedicated Multiplayer tab in Lens Explorer, with a guided colocation flow to establish a shared coordinate space.

Image: Snap for Developers

Screen mirroring app

Designed the phone-to-glasses casting experience for Spectacles — letting users bring any content from their phone into AR space and interact with it using hand gestures. The cast screen follows the user naturally without being distracting, can be anchored to the world or to the user's body, and returns into view automatically when it drifts out of frame.
